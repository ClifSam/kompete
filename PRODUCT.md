# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Startup founders and operators at early-to-growth-stage companies who need to understand their competitive landscape before pitching, pivoting, or planning. Secondary audience: Korean startup evaluators at AIP who assess this as a portfolio piece demonstrating product thinking and AI engineering.

Context: quick decision-making sessions — pre-meeting research, competitive positioning, strategic planning. Users are busy, analytical, and skeptical of fluff. They open Kompete when they need a fast, credible answer — not a polished consultant deck.

## Product Purpose

Kompete is a real-time AI competitive intelligence tool. A user enters a company name; an autonomous AI agent searches the web and streams a structured competitive brief — who the competitors are, how they position themselves, where the gaps are.

Success: the user walks away with actionable competitor intel in under two minutes, formatted well enough to share or reference directly. The value is speed × credibility — faster than manual research, more structured than a generic search.

## Brand Personality

Smart · Clean · Precise

Voice of a senior analyst who gives exactly what you need, no fluff. The interface disappears into the work. Competent without being cold; confident without being loud.

## Anti-references

- **AI hype aesthetic** — dark backgrounds with neon glows, ChatGPT-wrapper vibes, blue-purple gradients with particle effects. Looks cheap and generic. Kompete is a professional tool, not a tech demo.
- **Landing page template** — hero section with stock illustration, three identical feature cards, green gradient CTA button. Cookie-cutter SaaS that reads as disposable.
- **Purple-gradient startup** — the Notion/HubSpot clone aesthetic: pastel purples, rounded everything, friendly-but-shallow. Wrong register for a tool used in strategic decisions.
- **Enterprise grey** — dense, joyless, Salesforce-era dashboards. The opposite failure mode: technically correct, visually defeated.

## Design Principles

1. **The output is the interface** — The streaming analysis result is the product. Every design decision serves the legibility and credibility of that output. If a visual element doesn't help the user read, trust, or act on the data, it doesn't belong.
2. **Speed signals competence** — The UI communicates that the AI is working hard and fast. Streaming state is an experience, not a loading spinner. Show the work as it happens.
3. **Restraint earns trust** — Founders making strategic decisions need confidence, not delight. Use color sparingly. White space is not emptiness — it's margin for judgment.
4. **Transparency by default** — Show sources, steps, and structure. The agent's process builds credibility. Opacity hides value; legibility is a feature.
5. **Precise is not cold** — Smart doesn't mean sterile. Rhythm in spacing, weight contrast in typography, and small intentional touches make precision feel considered rather than clinical.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum throughout. All text must meet 4.5:1 contrast against its background. Streaming text must not flash or cause motion sickness — char-by-char animation is off by default. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support. Keyboard-navigable throughout.
